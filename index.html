Interesting Facts about Camels

  • There are two types of camels: One humped or “dromedary” camels and two humped Bactrian camels.
  • Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  • Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can't eat.

Interesting Facts about Chameleons

  • Unlike many lizards, chameleons can't regrow their tail.
  • Currently, there are around 200 different chameleon species, 44 percent of which can be found on Madagascar.
  • They mainly change color in order to communicate or regulate body temperature.

Interesting Facts about Cicadas

  • There are around 3,000 cicada species.
  • Their wings are transparent and can seem rainbow-hued when held up to a light source.
  • The buzzing sound they make is a mating call.

Interesting Facts about Pandas

  • Giant pandas are good at climbing trees and can also swim.
  • Pandas have carnivorous teeth, but they eat bamboo and fruit.
  • Pandas like to lick copper and iron.

Interesting Facts about Teddies

  • The Oxford English Dictionary dates the first use of the term teddy bear to 1906.
  • The incident of Roosevelt sparing the bear led to the popularity and naming of Teddy bears.
  • The origins of Hug A Bear Day are unclear but it may be connected with the day in November 1902 when Theodore “Teddy” Roosevelt refused to shoot a bear on a hunting trip in Mississippi.
